About

I'm a detail-oriented electrical power engineer working across power systems analysis, modelling and simulation. Most of my work sits at the intersection of network design and reliability — building models that tell you not just whether a system works, but how it fails, and what protects it when it does.

Alongside engineering practice, I teach. Over the past few years I've taught Power Stations, Switchgear & Protection, Electrical Energy Audit and High Voltage Engineering to diploma and undergraduate students, and run hands-on sessions in ETAP, MATLAB/Simulink and DIgSILENT PowerFactory.

My research centers on protection coordination for the Integrated Nepal Power System, and I've contributed to work on high-frequency transformer design for solid-state transformers.
